Mee Pok


Description

Mee pok is a vibrant, street-style noodle dish that’s all about bold flavors, springy texture, and soul-satisfying comfort. At its core, mee pok features flat, yellow egg noodles — firm, slightly chewy, and tossed until they gleam with a slick, flavorful sauce.

The sauce is the heart of it all: a savory, tangy, and spicy mix of black vinegar, soy sauce, garlic oil, chili paste, and sometimes a dash of lard for richness. Every strand of noodle is coated with attitude. Topping the noodles is a colorful medley: minced pork, fish balls, sliced fish cakes, and sometimes pork liver or a soft, marinated egg.

A sprinkling of crispy fried shallots, chopped scallions, and maybe a few pickled green chilies add punch and aroma. On the side (or poured over), a small bowl of clear pork or ikan bilis (anchovy) broth brings balance, light and umami-rich, to sip between bites. Mee pok is messy, spicy, slurpy, and unapologetically flavorful — a humble dish with a loud personality.
